# Abstract
This project investigates the effect of four scenarios projected for 2070 on pear yield
in a controlled experiment conducted in 24 ecotrons from Belgium and France. Each
ecotron unit was observed with two varieties (Conference and Doyenne du Comice), and
the climate scenarios were: no intervention (scenario 1), active CO2 removal (scenario
2), sustainable production, and sustainable transportation. Pear yield was modelled
with a Poisson Generalised Linear Mixed Model which included climate scenario, vari-
eties, and location as fixed effects and random effect for the ecotron. Climate scenario
had a highly significant effect on yield (χ2(3) = 47.02, p < 0.001). The CO2-removal
scenario produced the highest mean yield (26.0 pears/tree), representing a 71% increase
over the no-intervention baseline (rate ratio = 1.71, p < 0.001), while the sustainable
energy scenario yielded 32% more than the baseline (rate ratio = 1.32, p < 0.001).
The transportation scenario was indistinguishable from the control (p = 0.985). These
results, consistent with a parallel analysis of pear quality, identify CO2 removal as the
most beneficial climate intervention for pear production and growth. An exploratory
multivariate analysis giving insights on the role of soil as a mediator between climate
and production is also presented.
Key Words: ecotron units; Poisson GLMM; climate change; pear production; soil me-
diator.
